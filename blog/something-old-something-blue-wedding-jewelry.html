Something Old, Something Blue — Vintage Jewelry for the Bride

July 2026
← Back to Journal

The tradition is older than most people realise. "Something old, something new, something borrowed, something blue" dates to Victorian England — a rhyme that assigned each item a specific meaning. Something old for continuity with the past. Something blue for fidelity and love. Together they formed a kind of charm, a gathering of good intentions for the day ahead.

Vintage jewelry solves both at once. A genuine vintage piece is old by definition — it carries real history, real craft, a story that predates the wedding it will attend. And blue has always been the colour of bridal jewelry's most meaningful stones: sapphire, aquamarine, blue topaz, the particular blue of rhinestone glass that mid-century jewelers coaxed into something close to magic.

Seven pieces, from a $12 pair of clip-on earrings to an 18k Italian aquamarine bracelet. All genuinely vintage. All genuinely blue. Something old and something blue, found.

A vintage piece worn on a wedding day carries two stories — its own, and yours. That's the point.

Why vintage is the perfect something old and something blue

The wedding tradition asks for something old because continuity matters — the sense that you are joining something larger than a single day, that love and commitment have existed before you and will continue after. Vintage jewelry carries that continuity literally. Every piece above was made decades ago, worn or held by someone before you, and has arrived at this moment with its own quiet history.

Choosing vintage for your something blue also means choosing something genuinely irreplaceable. A mass-produced blue rhinestone necklace from a bridal accessories retailer will look like every other mass-produced blue rhinestone necklace. A signed Carl Art necklace in 12k gold filled with aqua rhinestones, or a solid 9ct gold ring with a genuine sapphire, or an estate Italian aquamarine bracelet — these are pieces that no one else will have.

The something blue pieces above range from $12 to $340. Whatever your budget, there is a genuine vintage option here that will mean more on the day — and more in the years after it — than anything bought new.
